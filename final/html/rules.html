Object of the Game

01

Claim your routes!

Claim your destination ticket routes on the gameboard using your train cards.

02

Get the most and longest train pathways!

Complete the most destination tickets and create the longest train path on the board!

03

Win!

When one player's stock of colored plastic trains gets down to only 0, 1 or 2 trains left at the end of his turn, each player, including that player, gets one final turn. The player with the highest score wins!

Set Up

01

Place Board and Pieces

Place board in center of table and place each players scoring marker on the start spot. Each player then takes the corresponding color of trains to their start piece.

02

Dealing out Cards

There are three different kinds of cards you will see. Train Cards, Destination Tickets, and the Longest Continuous Path card (refer to Game Pieces to learn about the different cards and pieces). Place the Longest Continuous Path card next to the board.
Deal out 4 Train Cards to each of the players. Take remaining train cards and lay 5 of them face up next to the board for all players to see. Set remaining train cards face down in pile next to the 5 upward facing train cards.

03

Destination Tickets

Deal out 3 Destination Tickets to each player. The player then must look at the Destination tickets and choose which ones they want to complete (must keep at least 2, may keep all 3 if wanted). These cards are kept the remainder of the game whether the player has completed them or not.

Board Set Up


On Your Turn

On your turn, you may pick ONE of the following actions

01

Draw Train Car Cards

Draw 2 Train Car cards: 2 from the top of the face down pile, 2 from the face up cards, or one from each. If there is a rainbow locomotive card face up and you choose to draw it, that is the only card you may draw. If you draw a rainbow locomotive card when blind drawing from the pile, you may proceed to draw a second card. If you draw any card from the face up cards, you must immediatly replenish that card after drawing from the face down (blind) pile. Once completed, your turn is now over.

02

Play Train Car Cards

If you can complete a connection between two cities using your train cards, you may play those cards into a discard pile and then place the trains on the respective path. This must be done all in one turn, cannot break up a connection between two cities into more than one turn. i.e. It takes 4 red train car cards to connect two cities, you must play all 4 cards in one turn. You may substitue rainbow locomotive cards in for a single colored train car. You can only play one connection per turn, so if you can connect multiple cities at a time, you must choose one to play. Once completed, your turn is now over.

03

Draw Destination Tickets

You may draw 3 Destination Tickets from the top of the deck. You must keep at least one of the cards if you choose to draw, you may keep all three if you so choose. Any returned cards get put in the bottom of the Destination Tickets deck. Remember, Destination Tickets that are uncompleted at the end of the game will count against you! Once completed, your turn is now over.
